1 Introduction  
The hydrogen atom, consisting of a single proton and an electron, is one of  
the fundamental systems in quantum mechanics and electromagnetism. Its  
simplicity allows for analytical solutions to the Schro¨dinger equation, making  
it a cornerstone of atomic physics.  
In classical terms, the hydrogen atom can be described by the relative  
position of the electron with respect to the proton. This is represented by  
the position vector r, which extends from the proton (at the origin) to the  
electron. Additionally, the center of mass (COM) of the system provides a  
useful reference point for analyzing motion in a two-body system.
